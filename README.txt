PART 1: CONTENT (Answer ALL questions, add your own if necessary)

1. What is the name of your business?
The name of the business will be “Green Guy’s Green Games.”
2. Is the business real or fictional?
The business is fictional.
3. What products or services does the business offer?
This business offers video game recommendations, but only if the game itself has the color green present in any way, big or small.
4. Who is the target audience?
The target audience would be people who like gimmick websites which they might find funny.
5. What problem does this business solve?
This business solves the problem of the lack of good green game recommendation websites, that is how the website would present it. In reality, the website would solve the problem of boredom, because someone could check in once in awhile to see what new game the website has recommended, and the humorous reason as to why. It is similar to the gimmick news site “The Onion” but if it were for games and hyper specific to green.
6. What makes this business unique compared to competitors?
The thing that makes this business unique compared to competitors is its absurd premise, if someone heard of a website that only recommends green games, it would be hard not to at least look at it for one second.
7. What pages will your website include (e.g., Home, About, Services, Contact)?
The website will include the pages Home, About, Games, and Contact.
8. Write a short mission statement for the business.
“Green Guys Green Games’ mission is to help gamers find video games that proudly feature the color green. No matter how big or small, green is supreme.”
9. What tone should the website content have (professional, friendly, modern, etc.)?
The website should have a humorous tone, while pretending to be professional. It is going to take itself fully seriously, which is where the humor would originate from. The fact that something so absurd is being said with a completely professional tone.
10. What call-to-action do you want visitors to take?
The call-to-action I would want is just to just check in on the website once in awhile to cure your boredom for even one second. 
11. What information must be immediately visible on the homepage?
The most immediate thing that must be seen is the logo and a short summary of what the website is about, from there the user can explore.
12. Will the site include images, videos, or other media? Why?
The website will primarily include images, as it is important so show off the games and what green color is present. 
--------------------------------------------------


PART 2: DESIGN (Answer ALL questions, add your own if necessary)

1. What overall style will the website have (minimalist, corporate, creative, etc.)?
The style of the website will be creative with a lot of green theming, while keeping it minimalistic to reinforce that fake professionalism feeling.
2. What color scheme will you use and why?
The color scheme will be primarily the color green and different variations of green.
3. What fonts will you use for headings and body text?
I am not entirely sure which specific font, but I would look for something bold and wide to make the website stand out.
4. How will spacing and layout improve readability?
By having elements separated appropriately the specific things that need to be focused on individual will be clearer to the viewer. 
5. What visual hierarchy will guide the user’s attention?
On something like the homepage, how the hierarchy will go is the title being the most obvious thing to focus on along with the logo. Secondly it would be a short summary of the websites gimmick, then lastly the featured games will be next. Like this the person will already know the gimmick by the time they actually see the games.
6. How will consistency be maintained across pages?
The same typical hierarchy will be used across pages, but maybe on pages apart from the home page will have a smaller version of the title and logo. There will always be a title first and description second focus, whether it’s the games or the logo.
7. What icons will you use?
Some icons that will be used will be the logo, the stars for the games rating, and the covers for the games that will be included.
8. What emotions should the design evoke in users?
The emotion should be initial confusion as the user slowly realizes that this website is not just another game review site and is instead something completely different.
9. How will accessibility be considered (contrast, font size, etc.)?
All the text will always be big and clear, with the icons and game covers being big and prioritized in the layout and spacing.
10. What inspiration sources influenced your design?
Some inspirations are gimmick websites like https://userinyerface.com/ and The Onion. These websites all have funny gimmicks to cure any boredom the person might be experiencing in the moment.
12. Describe the layout of the homepage and build the wireframe
The homepage has a header with the logo, website name, and a tab for pages. Under that is an about section that explains the website. Below that is a games section that shows three featured green games with space for images and a star icon for reviews.
--------------------------------------------------
PART 3: INTERACTIVITY (Answer ALL questions, add your own if necessary)

1. What interactive elements will your site include (forms, buttons, menus, etc.)?
The website will include menus and buttons; it is mostly a website that is meant to be viewed than having an active involvement in. 
2. How will users navigate the website?
The users will navigate the website primarily through buttons which take you to different pages.
3. Will there be form validation or user feedback? Explain.
There will not be form validation because the website does not require users to submit forms. There may be simple user feedback, like a page for users to type game recommendations, and they can explain why they are good green games.
4. What JavaScript functionality will you implement?
JavaScript will be used for navigating the website and making certain elements work like clickable buttons.
5. How does interactivity improve user experience?
Interactivity will help the website by making it feel less dull and static. Going through the page and looking at the games can be fun as seeing what the “reviewer” says about the game could be interesting, as it will often be something funny.
